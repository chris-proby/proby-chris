## Post Copy (Initial Draft)

AI is progressing rapidly: Proby’s interview moderator has reached human-like evaluation levels in our internal benchmark—where the bar is “evaluated as human” by blind assessors.

This is a meaningful step for AI-led qualitative research. Our benchmark uses a 10-point scale across nine dimensions: domain knowledge, improvised probing, question diversity, error and surprise handling, mistakes and missed signals (fewer is better), participant trust, and research value. The cutoff is 5: above 5 is classified as human-like, 5 or below as AI-like. Human moderators in our dataset consistently sit in the 6–9.5 range; typical AI moderators land in the 2–4.5 range.

When we started tracking in early 2026, Proby’s interviews were mostly evaluated as AI. By March 2026, we saw a clear shift: Proby’s sessions are now consistently evaluated as human—on par with human moderator sessions in the same benchmark. That’s the largest single-period gain we’ve seen on this human-likeness metric.

The benchmark is built on real interview transcripts (IDIs, dyads, and topic-specific studies) and is designed to reflect what actually matters in fieldwork: adaptive probing, handling unexpected answers, and keeping participants comfortable enough to give research-grade insights.

We’re sharing this not to claim we’ve “solved” human moderation, but to show that AI interview systems can be measured rigorously—and that with the right design and iteration, they can cross into the range where evaluators can’t reliably tell them apart from humans.
