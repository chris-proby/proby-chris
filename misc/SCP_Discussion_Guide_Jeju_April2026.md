# **Discussion Guide — Jeju Corridor**

Research Objectives: 

1. **Understand how KR travelers search for and evaluate pensions for Jeju trips** in detail – what are the filters, categories, information sections and social proof they rely on? 

2. **Identify where Airbnb falls short compared to competitors** \- specific information gaps and UX friction points on Airbnb’s pages specific to Jeju pensions

3. **Diagnose whether the Experiences & Services bundle offer is visible** in natural booking flow, and whether the **Experiences & Services supply in Jeju is compelling enough** to drive coupon redemption

---

**Section 2: Trip Planning & Accommodation Priorities  \[15 min\]**

**OBJECTIVE:** Understand the priorities for a Jeju trip & the overall ‘journey’ of going to Jeju.

* **Tell me about your upcoming Jeju trip. What is the occasion, and what are you most looking forward to?**

  →  Who are you going with? How did Jeju come up as the destination?

  →  How far in advance did you start looking into this trip?

* **What would need to be true about the trip to call it a successful one?** 

  →  Any must-do’s or must-haves? e.g. stay (e.g. pension), the area, amenities (e.g. BBQ), price, activities, or something else?

* **Walk me from the beginning of planning this Jeju trip, from the very first step. What do you research or sort out first, and what comes later?**

  →  What are the elements of this trip, and what is the sequence of planning/booking → flights, accommodations, activities, car rental, restaurant reservations, what else? 

* **What kind of accommodation would you like to stay in?** 

  →  Why did you decide to stay in a pension? What is it about a pension stay that you can’t get in other types of accommodations? 

  →  What steps have you taken to search for accommodations? What will you do next? (e.g. discuss with travel companions, visit xxx site/app, check prices, read blogs/reviews?) 

  →  Is there anything that matters specifically for a Jeju trip that you would not think about as much for other destinations?

* **Now, thinking about pensions specifically, what are the most important factors to consider when selecting which pension to stay at?**  

  →  What are the most important factors to consider when selecting your accommodations? 

* **Location** – what specific location needs do you have and why are they important?  
* **Price** – what is the budget in mind? How do you find a suitable price? (e.g. loyalty points, discounts, cross-compare with platforms to find the best price, what else?)   
* **Quality \-** What signals would you use to assess quality? (e.g. photos, reviews)  
* **Comfort, Cleanliness \-** What signals would you try to find to predict the comfort?   
* **Amenities** \- What kind of amenities are important for this trip? Why? Are they MUST-haves?  
* **What** **else**? 

* **Do you have an idea of a ‘type’ of pension you’d like to stay in?**

  →  What about gamsung/감성 pensions? What makes a pension "gamsung" in your view? Are you looking to stay in one \- and how would you search for it? 

**Section 3: Competitor Walkthrough (Naver/Yanolja/Goodchoice/Agoda)  \[25 min\]**

**OBJECTIVE:** Observe the full search-to-evaluation flow on a competitor. Questions are grouped by theme tags so you can follow the participant's lead and jump to whichever theme surfaces first.

* **Now, please show me how you would search for a \*pension\* for your upcoming trip to Jeju. Feel free to start on whichever app or website you would usually use. As you start searching, please think out loud and talk me through what you are doing.**  

  →  Why did you start on that platform?

  →  Is \[xxx\] the platform you always book with? What makes them top-of-mind for you? 

**●  HOMEPAGE, SEARCH AND LISTING CARDS**

* **\[Homepage\] I see a lot of different recommendations, categories and promotions here. Do you usually look at them?** 

  →  How relevant are they for you?

  →  What kinds of campaigns or promotions would catch your eye / do you usually look for?

  →  What kinds of ‘categories’ or ‘recommendations’ are useful? Would you click on them or are they more for inspiration? Under what conditions would you actually click on them? 

* **\[Search bar\]** 

  → **OBSERVE:** if they type a search term or ‘tap’ on the drop-down list of destinations. What is the preference between tapping on the destinations list v.s. typing a search term? 

  → **OBSERVE:** what search terms they use, whether they type a specific area of Jeju or just "Jeju,". If they use a more specific term or location, probe why? How did you know to search for that specifically? 


* **\[Pension Categories\]** 

  → **OBSERVE:** if they tap or filter to a specific pension category (e.g. pet-friendly, family-friendly, pool villa)

   →  Are there different "types" of pensions you think about, for example pool villas, family-friendly pensions, or gamsung pensions? **How do you define them? What are the pension ‘types’ in your mind?** 

   →  How important is it for this platform to ‘classify’ or ‘categorize’ at that level of pension types, vs. just a broader category of ‘pensions’? 

  Note to moderator: Many competitors have sub-categories of pensions, we are trying to evaluate if this is critical for the mental model of KR travelers. 


* **\[Filters\] Walk me through which filters you have applied and what each one is doing for you.**

  →  Which of these filters are deal-breakers for you, meaning you would not look at results without them? And which are more about narrowing down?

  →  Are there any improvements you can think of for these filters, or the way they are presented? Any missing ones?  

  →  If they skip filters entirely: I noticed you went straight to scrolling results without filtering. Is that typical for you? How do you narrow things down instead?


* **\[Search Results\] What do you think of these search results?** 

  →  Looking at the listing cards in the search results, what are the most important info you see here to help you decide which to click further into? 

  →  Check-in time → how important is seeing this information at this point? Why? 

  →  What other information would you wish to see on the listing cards? 

**●  LISTING DETAIL PAGE**

* **What made you tap into this listing? What caught your attention?**

* **Is the information you care about easy to find and clearly presented on this page?**

  **OBSERVE:** Note the order in which they scan the listing page. Do they scroll straight to photos, jump to amenities, check reviews, or look at price first?

  →  What information on this page makes you feel more or less confident about booking? 


* **\[Amenities\] Tell me about the amenities for this pension. Which ones matter to you, and how easy is it to find that information here?**

  →  For things like BBQ, pool, or Jacuzzi, is the information detailed enough? What information would you want to find? 

  → Would you need to check somewhere else to confirm? (e.g. naver blogs, reviews? What for?) 

  → Can you tell from this page how much it costs? Walk me through where you would find that. Is this information important to you? 

* **\[Page Structure\] How easy or difficult is it to find the information you need on this page?** 

  →  Is there anything you had to scroll a long way to find, or anything that was not where you expected it to be? E.g. Yanolja uses tabs and structured headings to organize PDP information. 


* **\[Multiple Rooms\] I see this pension offers different room types. Walk me through how you are comparing them.**

  →  How common is it for pensions to have multiple room types? Is being able to compare different room types something you already expect?  

  →  What elements are you focusing on when comparing the different room types?


* **\[Map\] I see you are looking at the map. What are you checking for? What information are you trying to get from it?**

  →  What exact information on the map do you look for? (e.g. points of attraction, beach, supermarket?) 

  →  Do you already have a specific area of Jeju in mind, or is the map helping you decide where to stay?

  →  When would you use the map inside \[xxx\] versus Naver Maps? What information do you expect here on this platform, versus what you will check later on on Naver?   


* **\[Reviews\] Walk me through how you are reading these reviews. What are you looking for?**

  →  What kind of review content gives you the most confidence? Is it the written detail, the photos, the overall rating, or something else?

  →  What would make photo reviews useful for your evaluation? (e.g. number of reviews, categories of photos, quality of photos?)

  →  If video reviews are available: Do you watch these? What makes a video review worth your time vs. one you would skip?

* **Would you try to find reviews of pensions anywhere else before booking? For example, Naver Blog, Instagram, or YouTube? Why?**

  →  How important is it for accommodation platforms to show photo or video reviews, if you are also using external social media to research these listings?


* **\[Price\] Talk me through how you are evaluating the price of this listing. What are you factoring in?**

  →  When you compare prices, are you comparing what you see on the listing page, or are you adding up the total cost including things like BBQ fees or cleaning charges?

  →  What is your preference of paying for these extra charges directly when booking in the app, vs during your stay to the host directly?

* **\[Discounts/Rewards\] What do you think about the promotions or rewards on this page? Do they influence your decision?** 

  →  How do loyalty points (e.g. naver points, NOL points), rewards or cashback factor in? How do you calculate what the final “value” of the place is?

  →  If they mention coupons: Do you actively search for coupons before booking, or do you rely on whatever the platform shows you? How would the flow of finding coupons be?

* **\[Amenities\] What type of amenities information are you trying to find? Can you find them easily on Airbnb?**

  → **OBSERVE:** Many times the host writes the cost for these in the unstructured paragraph section. To observe whether participants find this an issue

  →  If the cost is not clear, what would you do next? (e.g. find another listing, contact host, check another platform?) 


* **\[Page Structure\] How easy or difficult is it to find information on this page?**  

  →  If you could reorganize the page, what would you move or change to make it easier to find the information you need? 


* **\[Multiple Rooms\] Can you tell if this pension has different room types?**   

  → **OBSERVE:** Currently, Airbnb shows each room as 1 separate listing. There is no easy way to check out other rooms in the same pension property (the only path is to go to the Host profile to see if they have other listings).   

  →  When you reviewed pensions on \[competitor\], did you typically compare different room types within the same property? Is that something you thought about here?


* **\[Maps\] How is the map experience on Airbnb? What information are you getting from it?**    

  **OBSERVE:** How are they using the map differently on Airbnb vs competitor platform? 

  →  How easy or difficult is it to determine the location context of the pension? What are the difficulties? 

  →  The map here uses Google Maps. How sufficient is the location context from this map? What would you still need to check on Naver Maps separately?


* **\[Reviews\] How do the reviews on Airbnb compare to what you saw on \[xxx\]? Walk me through what you notice.**

  **OBSERVE:** Note: do they spend more or less time on reviews here? Do they look for photo reviews specifically?

  →  For this listing specifically, do you feel you have a good sense of the listing quality from these reviews? If not- what is missing? Where would you go to look up more reviews?

  →  What would make the reviews on Airbnb more useful for you?

* From who? (e.g. reviews from Koreans only, review from couple travellers)   
* Format (e.g. photos, videos)  
* Categorization (e.g. structured by theme?)   
* Content (e.g. longer reviews?)  
* Quantity (how many reviews needed to feel confident?) 

  →  If Airbnb wanted to invest more in photo/video reviews, what advice would you give to make it more useful for Korean travelers?

* **\[Pricing\] Looking at the pricing, how does this compare to what you saw for similar pensions on \[xxx\]?**

  →  What differences do you notice? (e.g. price for default number of guests / total guests) 

  →  What questions do you have about the pricing displayed on Airbnb? Is anything unclear?  

  →  Does Airbnb feel like it is offering comparable value compared to \[xxx\]? What would Airbnb need to offer you to feel like it’s better value than the alternatives?

  →  What is the single biggest thing Airbnb would need to improve to win you over for a Jeju pension booking?
